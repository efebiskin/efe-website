I · The Craft

A slower
fire.

Our distillery has stood, unchanged in essentials, for one hundred and thirty-eight years. The same spring, the same two copper stills, the same unreasonable patience.

We run every batch three times slower than most modern distilleries. Our wash still holds a gentle simmer for six hours where others push through in two. It is inefficient by any commercial measure.

It is the only way to make a single malt we are willing to put our name on.

01

Water

Sourced from the Avon burn, filtered through four hundred feet of Cairngorm granite. Soft. Mineral. Cold year-round.

02

Barley

Floor-malted at Balmuirfield since 1924. Every grain walked by hand across the malting floor twice a day for seven days.

03

Casks

First-fill Oloroso sherry butts, sourced from a single family bodega outside Jerez. We have never bought on the open market.

04

Time

Minimum fifteen winters under slate roofs. Our cellar master's only instruction since 1994: "Do not rush the cask."

III · Heritage

One hundred and
thirty-eight winters.

  1. 1887

    The first cut

    Alasdair Hume signs the lease on a disused corn mill on the banks of the Avon and begins distilling under the name Ignis.

  2. 1924

    Balmuirfield Maltings

    The distillery begins floor-malting its own barley. The practice has never stopped.

  3. 1958

    The silent decade

    Production halts for eleven years during family disputes. The warehouses remain full. This is where our oldest expressions were born.

  4. 1994

    Cellar master's rule

    Morag Hume becomes cellar master and issues the standing instruction: "Do not rush the cask."

  5. 2019

    Independent forever

    The family trust rejects the seventh acquisition offer and signs a charter binding the distillery to remain independently owned.

  6. 2026

    A slower fire, still

    Three generations in. The stills are the same. The rule is the same. The answer is the same.
